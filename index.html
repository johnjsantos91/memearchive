Brief history of Political Caricatures

When it started?

Political caricatures can be traced in history from 1732 with William Hogarth's "A Harlot's Progress".
Political caricatures make social criticism through acid humor on political issues like abuse of power, corruption, and ineffective management of political leaders.

Political Memes

Memes are part of everyday media consumption. Through humor, just like political caricatures, they evoke reality and different viewpoints from a variety of demographic groups.
Images and texts change depending on the message they want to convey.
Political memes have become a strong medium of propaganda, having the advantage of being anonymous.
This aspect gives total freedom of speech, triggering a POLITICAL MEME WAR.
